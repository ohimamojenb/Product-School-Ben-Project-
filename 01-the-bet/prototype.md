## What I Built
<!-- One sentence: what does this prototype demonstrate? --> An AI Oracle feature for the SME CRM (ChurnGuard) that flags which customers are at risk of churning and explains why, based on contact and purchase pattern changes.

## AI Value Archetype
<!-- Automator / Copilot / Oracle / Creator / Orchestrator --> Oracle — it surfaces a hidden signal (churn risk) the SME owner can't easily compute themselves from raw contact/purchase data, and that signal directly drives an action (who to call).

## The Bet in One Sentence
<!-- What you're building, for whom, why now --> SME owners in Nigeria lose customers silently because they have no systematic way to notice a customer has gone quiet until it's too late — an AI that flags churn risk early, with a clear reason, lets them intervene while there's still a relationship to save.


## Kill Criteria
<!-- When would you stop? What evidence would kill this bet? --> If SME owners say they already know intuitively which customers have gone quiet without needing a tool to tell them, the bet is dead. It's also dead if the churn flags don't lead to any actual follow-up behavior change, or if the "why" explanation doesn't feel more useful than just sorting customers by last contact date manually — i.e. if the AI isn't adding insight beyond what a simple filter would already show.
