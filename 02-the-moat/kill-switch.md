## Portability Score
<!-- Ready / Partial / Locked --> Equities execution has a real fallback path through the direct NGX integration, which meaningfully reduces our exposure to Infoware-specific downtime. However, we remain fully locked on two fronts: there is no alternative provider for NGX order execution itself (regulated monopoly, fallback routing doesn't change this), and NIBSS/NIP has zero redundancy for wallet funding and transfers. We've moved from total lock-in to a mixed position, stronger on routing resilience, still exposed on provider concentration and on the wallet-funding rail entirely.

## If [primary vendor] doubles pricing tomorrow:
<!-- What's your 48-hour response? --> On the NGX side specifically, having a direct integration doesn't help with pricing, NGX is still the only regulated venue for Nigerian equities settlement regardless of which path (Infoware or direct) we use to reach them, so we'd have no real negotiating leverage there. If the price increase came from Infoware specifically as a middleware fee rather than an NGX fee, the direct NGX integration gives us genuine leverage, we could credibly threaten to shift more volume through the direct path, which we couldn't say believably before. Our 48-hour response would be to clarify which entity is actually raising prices, escalate to Group finance and leadership either way, and where the direct NGX path applies, use it as real negotiating leverage rather than just an emergency fallback. On NIBSS, we'd have no leverage at all and would need to absorb cost short-term while evaluating fee pass-through to customers.

## If [primary vendor] ships a competing product:
<!-- What's defensible that they can't replicate? --> This remains lower risk, since NGX/Infoware operates at the infrastructure layer and has no natural mandate to compete with us directly for retail customers. What's defensible on our side is our SEC license combined with breadth across asset classes (T-Bills, FGN Bonds, Commercial Papers, not just equities), our position inside the FSDH Group structure giving us the Unified Wealth Dashboard advantage, and now also our direct NGX relationship itself, which is a genuine asset, not just a fallback, since it means we're not entirely dependent on Infoware's interpretation or packaging of the NGX relationship if they ever tried to move closer to the customer layer.
